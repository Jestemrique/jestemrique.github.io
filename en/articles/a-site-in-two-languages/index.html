A site in two languages

April 15, 2026

I wanted the site to be in Spanish and English, with each language on its own URL.

The scheme I chose

Each language lives in its own folder and carries its code in the address:

  • Spanish → /es/
  • English → /en/

The default language is Spanish, so the root of the site redirects to /es/.

The hard part

The challenge wasn't having two languages, but linking each page to its translation for the language switcher. When the addresses in both languages don't match (for example /es/articulos/ and /en/articles/), you have to tell the site explicitly that they are the same page in another language.

What matters isn't just translating the content, but letting the visitor jump from one version to the other without getting lost.

Solving it taught me a lot about how a multilingual site works under the hood.
